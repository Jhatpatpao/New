About Bhagwan Buddha

Gautam Buddha, also known as Siddhartha Gautama, was a spiritual teacher and the founder of Buddhism. Born in Lumbini (present-day Nepal) in 563 BCE, he was raised as a prince but renounced his royal life to seek enlightenment. After years of meditation and asceticism, he attained Nirvana under the Bodhi tree in Bodh Gaya. His teachings focus on the Four Noble Truths and the Eightfold Path, emphasizing compassion, mindfulness, and the cessation of suffering.
